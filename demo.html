The Problem

We are more connected online than ever — and more isolated next door than ever before.

The Shift

So we built something with no reels, no likes, no popularity contest — only people helping people.

Who Needs It

Help is usually invisible. We only notice it during a flood, a heatwave, a crisis on the news, when in truth vulnerable neighbors Who this includes
  • Elderly individuals living alone
  • People with limited mobility
  • Those recovering from illness or surgery
  • New parents managing alone
  • Anyone temporarily unable to get by without support
 need it quietly, every single day. Beacon of Unity is built so anyone, not just during emergencies, can be seen and helped.

Verified Trust

Every neighbor, confirmed.

Every user verifies through Emirates ID before they can post or respond — so help requests come from real people you can trust, not anonymous accounts.

Ask & Answer

Help, without the friction.

Post a request, set the urgency, and let nearby neighbors respond — whether it's a task, an errand, or something urgent. A digital lifeline for seniors and anyone living alone.

Lost & Found

Reunited, faster.

A centralized, community-run lost-and-found — so a missing wallet, pet, or set of keys finds its way home through the people around it.

The Thank You Wall

Kindness, made visible.

A gamified points system and a public Thank You Wall turn generosity into something celebrated — rewarding compassion instead of popularity.
